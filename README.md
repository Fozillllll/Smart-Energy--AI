# Smart-Energy--AI
Building AI course project

## Summary
The **Smart Energy AI** project aims to optimize household and industrial energy consumption using machine learning. By analyzing historical usage patterns and external factors like weather, the system predicts future energy needs and suggests ways to reduce waste and lower electricity bills.

## Background
* **Problem:** Global energy demand is rising, leading to high costs and environmental strain. Many people waste energy simply because they don't know when or how they are over-consuming.
* **Frequency:** This is a daily problem for millions of households and businesses worldwide.
* **Motivation:** My motivation is to combine AI technology with sustainability to create a tool that helps both the environment and people's finances.
* **Importance:** Efficient energy use is a key step toward a greener planet and sustainable living.

## Data and AI techniques
* **Data Sources:** 
    - Historical smart meter data (hourly or daily usage).
    - Local weather data (temperature, humidity).
    - Energy price data (if available).
* **AI Techniques:**
    - **Linear Regression or LSTM (RNN):** To predict future energy demand based on time and weather.
    - **Anomaly Detection:** To identify unusual spikes in energy usage that might indicate faulty appliances.
    - **Clustering:** To group users with similar patterns for personalized saving tips.

## How is it used?
The solution can be integrated into a mobile app or a web dashboard. 
* **Users:** Homeowners, building managers, and factory operators.
* **Impact:** It helps users visualize their consumption and receive real-time notifications on how to save energy (e.g., "Turning down your heater by 1 degree now could save you 5% this month").

## Challenges
* **Limitations:** The AI cannot physically turn off old, inefficient appliances. It also depends heavily on the availability of high-quality data from smart meters.
* **Privacy:** Handling personal energy data requires strict security measures to protect user privacy.

## What next?
In the future, this project could grow into a fully automated **Smart Home AI** that communicates directly with IoT devices (like smart thermostats and lights) to manage energy automatically without user intervention.
